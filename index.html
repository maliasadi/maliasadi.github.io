I am Ali, a PhD student in Computer Science. My research is focused on high-performance scientific computing under the supervision of Professor Marc Moreno Maza at the University of Western Ontario, Canada.



Interests

My background and research interests have been centered on cryptography and data security, software development, and parallel programming since 2013. Currently, I am focused on developing high-performance software for computer algebra, that includes:
    • Low-level programming for algebraic computation
    • Design and analysis of data structures
    • Parallel patterns and algorithms

Biography

My Ph.D. began with C implementation of algorithms over sparse multivariate polynomials from scratch in the Basic Polynomial Algebra Subprograms (BPAS) library. To provide fast, optimized, and parallel software for solving polynomial systems (e.g., Curves, Surfaces) with the theory of RegularChains, I have been part of design and developing the following projects:
    • Parallel Triangularize decomposition of polynomial systems
    • Zero-dimensional RegularChains
    • Sparse multivariate polynomials over integers (SMZP) and rational numbers (SMQP)
    • Dense univariate polynomials over prime fields (DUSP) and integers (DUZP)
Previously, I studied algorithmic efficiencies and new optimization of Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) over the integers with some experimental prototyping in SageMath (Python) as my master thesis under the supervision of RR. Farashahi. Prior to attending graduate school, I defended my bachelor technical report in applications of Elliptic Curve Cryptography in SSH, TLS, and Bitcoin cryptocurrency.
