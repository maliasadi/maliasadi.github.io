I am Ali, a PhD student in Computer Science. My research is focused on high-performance scientific computing under the supervision of Professor Marc Moreno Maza at the University of Western Ontario, Canada.


Biography

I am currently developing the Basic Polynomial Algebra Subprograms (BPAS) library, where my journey has been started with C implementation of advance algorithms over sparse multivariate polynomials from scratch, and now, I am in charge of design, implementation, and optimization of algorithms over Finite Fields and Algebraic Extensions. This provides fast, optimized, and parallel (with Multicore/Manycore supports) software for solving polynomial systems (e.g., Curves, Surfaces, and Lattices) with the theory of RegularChains.

In December of 2016, I defended my master thesis in Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) over the integers under the supervision of RR. Farashahi and A. Rejali. I studied algorithmic efficiencies and new optimization of DGHV's FHE with some experimental prototyping in SageMath (Python).

In September of 2014, I defended my bachelor thesis, which is a review of Elliptic Curve Cryptography schemes in SSH, TSL, and Bitcoin cryptocurrency protocols, under the supervision of RR. Farashahi.

My research interests are tied to cryptography and advanced software development, where you need a strong background in mathematics, statistics, and programming. I am focused now on developing high-performance software for computer algebra, that includes:
    • Computational Algebraic Geometry
    • Design and Analysis of Advanced Data Structures
    • Design Patterns and Analysis of Parallel Algorithms
    • Low-Level Programming for Algebraic Computation
